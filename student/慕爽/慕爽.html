2019 Project - Frequencing of interruptions for effective user's feedback quality

October 26 discussion notes

The quality of users' feedback affected by the frequency of interruptions and the kind of activities that are interrupted.

What affects the quality of users' feedback while on a mobile device and while conducting activities?

Outcome
  • Frequency and activities that don't compromise the quality of users' feedback.
  • Find out the best time to interrupt the user in order to get good quality answers for user testing
  • Use the data from the COAX tool
Hypothesis
Pop-up questionnaire

At specific or random times, The user could be asked, via a pop-up window, whether this specific time optimal for answering testing questions.
Users should be asked questions that just require a single interaction, so that they can be addressed even when they are busy.

  • Not right now
  • Ask me again in 5 minutes
  • Yes, I could answer your questions now.
  • (Dismiss the pop-up)

Schedule of questionnaire

The CAUX data provides information about several aspects of users' activities, such as apps being used and for how long, the motion state of the user, the location, etc. Scheduling questions at specific times and situations may help to learn from this users' feedback the optimal time when users' feedback quality is not compromised.

Preliminary testing

Preliminary testing is adviced for

  • The information on the pop-up
    • In this information clear?
    • Are these options that you can easily select and that would provide little or no distraction from relevant activities?
    • What are the times and activities when you would definitey not want to be interrupted with a pop-up like this one?
    • Please test this prototype, are the buttons easy to select? Is the contrast of the design, the font family and size, the layout easy to read?
Such information gathered during preliminary user researchand testing, allows for a successful designof the questionnaire as well as a successful initial pop-up schedule

Project abstract

Researchers are not always knowledgeable with the benefits and applications of data visualization. This tool would allow researchers to learn and adopt the most appropriate data visualization alternative when mapping specific data.

This project could be combined with Veronica's CAUX tool, which allows researchers to compile a CAUX research study as they go through as wizard-like interface.
